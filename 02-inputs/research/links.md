# Research bookmarks — The Demon Core

Seed bookmarks for the history deep-dive (vol1) and the replica build (vol2). Add sources as discovered; group by purpose.

## Primary historical sources

- [Demon core — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demon_core) — canonical synthesis; cites Hempelmann, Lushbaugh, McLaughlin et al.
- [Harry Daghlian — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Daghlian) — biography, August 21 1945 incident at Omega Site
- [Louis Slotin — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Slotin) — biography, May 21 1946 incident at Pajarito Site
- [Pit (nuclear weapon) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pit_(nuclear_weapon)) — Christy solid-pit design, δ-phase Pu-Ga alloy, nickel plating
- [Atomic Accidents — Nuclear Museum (Atomic Heritage Foundation)](https://ahf.nuclearmuseum.org/ahf/history/atomic-accidents/) — institutional summary, oral-history pointers
- [Medical Studies of the Demon Core Victims — Stanford PH241 (Granowitz 2024)](http://large.stanford.edu/courses/2024/ph241/granowitz2/) — dose modeling and outcome timelines
- ["The plutonium box" — *Restricted Data* (Alex Wellerstein, 2014-03-28)](https://blog.nuclearsecrecy.com/2014/03/28/plutonium-box/) — definitive piece on the Morrison-designed carrying case
- [Harold Agnew carrying the plutonium core, Nagasaki, 1945 — Rare Historical Photos](https://rarehistoricalphotos.com/harold-agnew-carrying-plutonium-core-nagasaki-fat-man-bomb-1945/) — context for the famous Agnew/Tinian photograph
- ["Twilight Time: A Soldier's Role in the Manhattan Project at Los Alamos" — Ralph C. Sparks (LAHS, 2000)](https://archive.org/details/twilighttimesold0000spar) — memoir of the machinist who built the boxes; full text on Internet Archive
